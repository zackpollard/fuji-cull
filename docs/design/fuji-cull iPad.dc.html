WHY — THE WAIT IS THE FIRST IMPRESSION

On the newest surface, the connect screen is a telemetry dashboard, not a splash. Numbered phases, a live percentage, throughput, and a scrolling engine log turn three minutes of unavoidable indexing into visible, trustworthy work.

WHY — CULL BEFORE IT FINISHES

Thumbnails stream in as they decode, so the grid is usable well before EXIF completes. The telemetry keeps running in the sidebar — honesty without a blocking gate.

WHY — SPLIT VIEW

The iPad’s width earns a persistent sidebar — the current card and its Immich albums always in reach, where phone hides them behind a header tap. The grid stays the star.

WHY — KEYBOARD + TOUCH, BOTH

With a Magic Keyboard attached, the exact desktop keymap works; without one, everything is tappable. The footer states both, and holding ⌘ shows the shortcut HUD (next frame).

Developer only. Hides the real camera and fills the grid with synthetic test shots. Turn this off before a real shoot.

WHY — FORM SHEETS, NOT BOTTOM SHEETS

iPad has the room for centered modal cards over a dimmed grid — the platform-idiomatic form-sheet. Phone uses bottom sheets because thumbs reach the bottom; iPad hands rest at the sides.

WHY — MAKE THE DANGER LOOK DANGEROUS

“Force fake corpus” can wipe a real session from view, so it is boxed in reject-red with a warning glyph and an explicit off-before-a-shoot note — visually quarantined from the safe toggles above.
